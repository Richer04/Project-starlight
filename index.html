Moon 2

Did you know that Earth has a second, mini-moon that occasionally pays us a visit? Known as a "quasi-satellite," this celestial companion is called 2020 SO. Discovered in September 2020, 2020 SO entered Earth's orbit but is not a natural moon; instead, it's likely a discarded rocket booster from a lunar mission launched in the 1960s.

Saturn V

One of the most powerful rockets ever built is the Saturn V, developed by NASA for the Apollo and Skylab programs. What's intriguing is the sheer scale of its engines. The five engines of the Saturn V's first stage, known as the F-1 engines, were the largest single-chamber liquid-fueled rocket engines ever used in operational service.

WASP-12b

One of the strangest planets ever discovered is known as "WASP-12b." This exoplanet, located about 1,400 light-years away from Earth, is so close to its host star that it's literally being consumed. WASP-12b is spiraling in towards its star, and the intense gravitational forces are causing the planet to stretch and deform into an egg-like shape.
